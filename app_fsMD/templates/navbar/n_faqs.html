Eligibility is determined by where you are physically located, your medical history, and whether telehealth is clinically appropriate for your needs. We also consider safety factors—like current medications, prior diagnoses, and any symptoms that may require in-person evaluation.

The process usually looks like this: you complete an intake form, confirm your location, and provide health information as accurately as possible. A U.S.-licensed clinician then reviews your details to decide whether FullScopeMD can safely care for you through telehealth.

If telehealth is not the right fit, we’ll tell you why and may recommend next steps (like an in-person visit, labs, or a different care setting).

Your care is provided by independently licensed U.S. clinicians (such as physicians, nurse practitioners, and physician assistants), authorized to provide care in the state where you are located at the time of your visit.

FullScopeMD supports the experience (intake, scheduling, messaging, and pharmacy coordination), while the clinician makes medical decisions using independent professional judgment.

Every treatment recommendation, prescription, and dose adjustment is evaluated for medical appropriateness, legal compliance, and patient safety.

Telehealth lets you receive care without traveling to a clinic. Depending on your situation, your care may include a video or audio visit, secure messaging, and review of information you submit (history, symptoms, photos, documents, or lab results).

After evaluation, your clinician may recommend a plan, request more information, or advise in-person evaluation if telehealth is not appropriate.

For details on telehealth benefits/risks and your responsibilities, read our Telehealth Informed Consent.

FullScopeMD supports multiple care paths, including weight management (such as GLP-1 programs where clinically appropriate), peptide therapy, skin health, hair-loss support, sick-visit style care, and everyday medical support.

Not every option is right for every patient. Your clinician will recommend a plan that makes sense clinically—sometimes including labs or an in-person evaluation before starting or continuing treatment.

FullScopeMD typically operates on a clear cash-pay model so you know costs up front. Pricing (consultations, programs, and medication if applicable) is shown before purchase whenever possible.

Some services may not be covered by insurance and we may not submit claims. If you plan to use insurance, confirm coverage with your insurer first. Many patients use eligible FSA/HSA funds when available.

Clinical services are generally non-refundable once rendered—meaning a licensed clinician has reviewed your intake, initiated a consultation, provided medical assessment, or made treatment decisions.

The full conditions are listed in our Refund Policy.

When medication is prescribed, it is prepared and shipped by fully licensed U.S. pharmacies. Proper preparation, handling, and shipping standards are part of patient safety.

Refund eligibility may change once a prescription is issued or fulfillment begins—see the Refund Policy.

After you submit information, it’s routed to the appropriate step: support triage, scheduling, or clinician review (depending on the form). If more information is needed, we may request clarification to keep care safe and accurate.

For general questions, start on our Contact page.

When FullScopeMD provides clinical services, certain data may qualify as Protected Health Information (PHI) under HIPAA. PHI is handled under HIPAA-aligned rules for permitted uses, disclosures, and safeguards.

Learn more in our Privacy Policy and HIPAA Notice of Privacy Practices.

Essential healthcare and operational messages (like appointment reminders, pharmacy updates, and security notices) may be required to use the Services. Marketing messages are optional and require explicit opt-in.

You can opt out of marketing emails using the unsubscribe link, and opt out of marketing SMS by replying STOP. For more information, see our Terms & Conditions.

Emergency Notice
FullScopeMD does not provide emergency or urgent medical care. If you believe you are experiencing a medical emergency, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room immediately.

For non-emergency questions or follow-ups related to your plan, contact support or schedule follow-up care when appropriate.
