HIPAA Notice

HIPAA NOTICE OF PRIVACY PRACTICES

Last Updated: December 5, 2025

This notice describes how medical information about you may be used and disclosed and how you can get access to this information.
Please review it carefully.

1. Our Responsibilities

FullScopeMD (“we,” “us,” “our”) is required by law to:

  • Maintain the privacy and security of your Protected Health Information (“PHI”).
  • Provide you with this Notice of our legal duties and privacy practices.
  • Notify you if a breach occurs that may compromise the privacy or security of your PHI.
  • Follow the practices described in this Notice unless we update it in writing.

Your PHI includes information that identifies you and relates to your past, present, or future health, treatment, or payment for services.

2. How We May Use and Disclose Your PHI

We may use or disclose your PHI in the following ways without obtaining your written permission:

A. Treatment

We may use and share PHI with physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, pharmacists, labs, and other healthcare professionals involved in your care.

Examples:
  • Reviewing your medical history
  • Conducting telehealth evaluations
  • Sending prescriptions to pharmacies
  • Ordering labs and reviewing results
B. Payment

We may use and disclose PHI to obtain payment for services.

Examples:
  • Processing payments
  • Determining coverage
  • Sending billing statements
C. Healthcare Operations

We may use PHI to manage our business and improve the quality of care.

Examples:
  • Quality assessments
  • Staff training
  • Customer service
  • Security and fraud prevention
D. Business Associates

We may share PHI with vendors who help us provide services (e.g., telehealth platforms, cloud hosting, pharmacies, labs). All business associates are required to protect your PHI through Business Associate Agreements (BAAs).

E. Required or Permitted by Law

We may share your PHI when required by federal, state, or local law, including:

  • Public health reporting
  • Abuse, neglect, or domestic violence reporting
  • Court orders or subpoenas
  • Law enforcement requests
  • Health oversight agencies
  • Workers’ compensation claims
F. To Prevent Serious Threats

We may use or disclose PHI to prevent or reduce a serious, immediate threat to health or safety.

3. Uses and Disclosures That Require Your Written Authorization

We must obtain your written authorization before using or disclosing your PHI for:

  • Marketing communications not otherwise permitted by HIPAA
  • Sale of PHI
  • Most psychotherapy notes
  • Any other use or disclosure not described in this Notice

You may revoke authorization at any time in writing, except to the extent PHI has already been used or disclosed.

4. Your Rights Regarding Your PHI

A. Right to Access

You may request to view or obtain a copy of your medical records. We will provide records in paper or electronic format as allowed by law.

B. Right to Amend

You may request corrections to your PHI if it is incorrect or incomplete. We may deny a request if:

  • We did not create the information
  • It is accurate as-is
  • Certain other legal exceptions apply
C. Right to Request Restrictions

You may request restrictions on how we use or disclose your PHI. We are not required to agree to most restrictions, except when:

  • You pay for a service completely out of pocket AND
  • You request that we not disclose that information to your health plan
D. Right to Request Confidential Communications

You may request that we contact you in a specific way (e.g., email only, or at a different phone number).

E. Right to an Accounting of Disclosures

You have the right to request a list of times we shared your PHI for six years prior to your request, excluding:

  • Treatment
  • Payment
  • Healthcare operations
  • Disclosures you authorized
F. Right to a Paper Copy of This Notice

You may request a paper copy of this Notice at any time, even if you have agreed to receive it electronically.

G. Right to Be Notified of a Breach

You have the right to receive written notification if a breach of your PHI occurs.

5. Telehealth-Specific Privacy Practices

  • Electronic transmission of PHI may occur through secure video, chat, photo uploads, or online forms.
  • We use encryption and HIPAA-compliant systems to protect PHI.
  • We do not record telehealth sessions unless required by law or explicitly authorized by you.

6. How We Protect Your Information

We use a combination of administrative, technical, and physical safeguards, including:

  • Encryption of PHI in transit and at rest
  • Multi-factor authentication
  • Access controls and audit logs
  • Secure servers and HIPAA-compliant platforms
  • Regular risk assessments

Although we use reasonable safeguards, no system can guarantee 100% security.

7. Changes to This Notice

We may update this Notice at any time. Updated versions will be posted on our website with a new “Last Updated” date. Your continued use of our services means you accept the updated Notice.
